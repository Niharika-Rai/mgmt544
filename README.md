# Healthcare Appointment Scheduling Database

This SQL project was implemented in GCP BigQuery. 

## Business Scenario & Problems Solved  

The Healthcare Appointment Scheduling Database is designed to streamline how hospitals, clinics, and medical centers manage patient appointments, doctor availability, medical services, and follow-up care. It serves as a centralized system that aligns patient preferences, such as preferred dates, times, and treatment types, with appropriate and available healthcare providers. The database ensures that both patients and doctors have access to essential information, including contact details, medical service descriptions, and treatment costs, enabling informed decision-making and smoother coordination. By integrating scheduling, communication, and service details into one platform, this system supports efficient healthcare delivery while enhancing patient convenience and overall accessibility.  

This database solves several operational and communication challenges commonly faced in healthcare environments. It prevents scheduling conflicts by matching appointments to real-time availability, ensures patients understand treatment costs ahead of time, and improves transparency between patients, doctors, staff, and healthcare administrators. The system also supports financial oversight through detailed payment tracking, helps executives evaluate performance and resource allocation, and enhances post-treatment care by storing prescriptions and follow-up information. Overall, it reduces administrative bottlenecks, increases scheduling efficiency, improves patient satisfaction, and provides leadership with clear insights for strategic decision-making. 

## Insightful Business Questions 

The Healthcare Database Queries.sql file contains the SQL queries that answer the questions below. I have also included a brief explanation of why and how the queries work to answer each of the questions. These business questions enable the database to solve some of the most pressing issues in the healthcare industry.   

1. Which doctors have the highest number of appointments in a given time period?

The main purpose of this query is to identify which doctors are in the highest demand. This is especially useful for workload balancing or performance reviews. Since our sample data reflected dates that were during the month of November, I wrote my query in a way that could help us identify the doctors with the most appointments, indicative of their higher demand, during the month of November in 2025. The query output shows the names of the doctors along with the total number of appointments they have had during the month of November, with the total number of appointments arranged in descending order. Hence, the top-most results would be the doctors with the highest demand since they would have the greatest number of appointments scheduled.  

2. What is the total revenue generated per medical service during a specific month? 

The main purpose of this query is to measure the monthly financial performance of each of the medical services offered during the most recent month, November. The query output shows the names of the medical services along with the total revenue generated by them. The total revenue depends on the payment status of the medical service offered and the specific amounts that the patients have paid for these services. This query output also helps us identify the most lucrative medical services offered from a business perspective, since the total revenue is arranged in descending order, and helps medical center owners to improve the operations of their business. It also helps them identify when patients have not paid for these services, indicating a potential affordability issue, which can help them make their healthcare services more patient-friendly.  

3. Which medical treatments (from MedicalService) are most frequently booked by patients? 

To identify which medical treatments are booked by patients, we took a look at the Appointment table with MedicalServiceID that included the treatment names of the patients. We joined the two tables and grouped each treatment name to track how many appeared for each patient. This is helpful in terms of allocating staff and equipment efficiently based on what service is in higher demand and not. 

4. What percentage of appointments are fully paid, partially paid, or unpaid? 

We calculated the percentage of the total paid, partial, and pending status for overall appointments by counting the total number of payments per patient. We can see the difference in percentage from the result query. This is very useful in detecting financial information for the hospital, so they can be aware of problems that indicate insurance, billing, and much more. 

5. Which departments handle the most appointments? 

In order to compare department performance, the following query was created to learn which Department has the most total appointments. This is an important metric in department performance because department efficiency can be gleaned from the total volume of appointments. This query orders by Total Appointments to serve as an easy way to find the top-performing departments by Name. This can aid in generating data-driven decisions to boost overall healthcare center efficiency.  

6. Which patients have visited the medical center most frequently over the last 6 months? 

This query is intended to identify loyal or high-need patients for targeted care programs or loyalty tracking. The output for the query shows the patient’s name, ID, and total healthcare visits, and is ordered by Total Visits to easily see which patients are the most important. Loyal customers help foster better relationships, which can lead to better care, which can be inferred from the query output. High-need patients need to be identified in order to anticipate future care, resource allocation, and long-term treatment planning. The information to identify high-need patients can be found in this query.  

7. How many appointments do not fall within the doctor’s availability? 

This query checks whether any appointments were scheduled outside of a doctor’s defined availability window. It joins the Appointment table with the DoctorAvailability table using DoctorAvailabilityID, then compares each appointment’s date and time against the availability’s date and start/end times. If an appointment’s time does not fall between the doctor’s start and end time, or if the appointment date does not match the availability date, it is counted as an appointment that does not fall within the doctor’s availability. The final result returns the number of such invalid appointments. 

8. What is the average treatment cost per department or doctor specialization? 

This query calculates the average treatment cost grouped by both doctor and department. It joins appointments, doctor availability, doctors, departments, and medical services, so the query can access each appointment’s associated medical service cost. After retrieving the treatment cost for each appointment, it computes the average cost per doctor and department combination. The grouped results show how treatment pricing varies across different doctors and departments, ordered from highest to lowest average cost. 

9. How many prescriptions were issued per doctor or per department within a date range? 

This query is designed to track prescribing activity by a doctor over a specific time period. It counts how many prescriptions each doctor issued between two dates (in this example, the month of November 2025, which matches our sample data). This helps the medical center identify doctors with higher prescribing workloads, potential prescription trends, and resource planning needs. By adjusting the date range in the WHERE clause, we can use the same query to monitor prescribing behavior across any time window. 

10. Which medical centers have the highest dispensing capacity, measured by how many pharmacies they own?

This query identifies which medical centers have the largest pharmacy footprint. It joins medical centers with their associated pharmacies and counts how many pharmacies each center operates. A higher number of pharmacies can indicate higher potential patient traffic and prescription volume, since these centers can serve patients across more pharmacy locations.
